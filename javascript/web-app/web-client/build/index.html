Precipitate is a web installation and performance that explores the acoustic qualities of weather through machine listening and re-sonification. People anywhere in the world are invited to lend their smartphones to extract sonic features from the weather in their local environment and listen to re-sonifications of that data. During a performance of Precipitate, live-streamed sound features will be used as material for musical improvisation performed in front of a live audience. Please review the following instructions before participating. These instructions can be returned to at any time.

Setup
  • Bring your phone and headphones to an area where you have internet access and can hear sounds created by weather (rain, hail, thunder, etc...).
  • Please do not enter dangerous weather areas for this piece. Weather can be listened to from a window or sheltered area!
Listening
  • Plug in your headphones (to avoid feedback) and hit 'start' to begin listening to a re-sonification of your local weather environment.
  • Your phone will calculate six different values that characterize the sound picked up by your phone. Live visualizations of the six features can be viewed under the 'Visualizer' tab.
  • You can listen to live re-sonifications of other participants' local weather environments by navigating to the 'Map' tab and tapping on a point on the map.
Sharing
  • Hit 'Share' to allow users to listen to re-sonifications of your weather environment.
  • Your phone will stream the extracted sound features of your immediate environment (not the sound itself) to the website for other users to listen to.
  • When you begin sharing, your approximate location (ie. about nearest city/town) will also be shared on the website.
  • At any moment you cant hit 'Stop Sharing' to remove your position from the map and stop streaming sound features to the server.

Cumulonimbus

Cumulonimbus is an interactive web installation and performance that seeks to explore the acoustic qualities of weather phenomena through machine listening and re-sonification.

People anywhere in the world are invited to lend their smartphones to extract sonic features from the weather in their local environment and listen to a re-sonification of that data. You can choose to listen to re-sonifications of other active users' weather through a map interface. During a performance of Cumulonimbus, live-streamed sound features will be used as material for musical improvisation performed in front of a live audience and streamed back to your smartphone.

Cumulonimbus seeks to foster a greater appreciation for, and understanding of, the elusive phenomena of global weather systems through participatory sound art.

Please review the following instructions before participating. These instructions can be returned to at any time.

Setup

  • Bring your phone and headphones to an area where you have internet access and can hear sounds created by weather (rain, hail, thunder, or a quieter ambiance of a sunny or snowy day).
  • Please do not enter dangerous weather areas for this piece. Weather can be listened to from a window or sheltered area, and more dramatic weather sounds do not necessarily translate to more spectacular re-sonifications!

Listening

  • Plug in your headphones (to avoid feedback) and select from the 'Listen' drop-down menu the source you would like to listen to.
  • You can listen to 3 different re-sonifications:
    1. Listen to a re-sonification of your own weather environment. Your phone will extract sonic features of the weather in your immediate environment and resynthesize new sounds from them.
    2. Listen to a re-sonification of another user's weather environment. Each point on the map corresponds to an active user in that approximate location, tap on a point to hear a re-sonification of the features streamed by that user.
    3. If a performance of Cumulonimbus is in progress somewhere in the world, you can listen to a live-performed stereo re-sonification of the weather data shared by other connected users.

Sharing

  • Hit 'Share' to allow users to listen to re-sonifications of your weather environment.
  • Your phone will stream the extracted sound features of your immediate environment (not the sound itself) to the website for other users to listen to, and for the performer (if a performance is in progress) to use as material for musical improvisation.
  • When you begin sharing, your approximate location will also be shared on the website. A point will appear on the map with a wider radius of your location such that your position within a city cannot be determined.
  • Extracted sound features that are streamed to the website will be archived in a database to be re-sonified in later performances.
  • Participation is completely anonymous so once data has been streamed it cannot be retrieved or erased from the database.
  • At any moment you cant hit 'Stop Sharing' to remove your position from the map and stop streaming sound features to the website.
